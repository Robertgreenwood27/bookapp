## Part 1: The Morning Ritual

The alarm's shrill cry pierced the darkness at 5:30 AM. Arys' eyes snapped open, bloodshot and burning, his hand already moving to silence the noise. He lay still for a moment, breathing deeply, feeling the weight of existence settling onto his chest like an unwelcome guest.

*Chirp. Another day. Swipe. Click. Here we go again. Thoughts cascade through my mind, shards of glass cutting through the fog of sleep. Each one a reminder of the monotony ahead, the endless cycle of calls and complaints. Eighty voices waiting to chip away at my sanity. Eighty opportunities for something to crack, for the darkness lurking beneath my skin to seep out.*

A soft groan escaped him as he swung his legs over the side of the bed. The floorboards creaked under his feet as he made his way through the darkness, muscle memory guiding him through the familiar space.

In the bathroom, Arys faced his reflection in the mirror. Disheveled dark hair framed a face etched with discontent. But it was his skin that drew his gaze, pale as a corpse and shot through with black veins. They seemed to writhe beneath the surface, a network of dark rivers carrying something far more insidious than blood.

*Look at yourself. Pale, drawn, a husk of a human being. Those veins... are they darker today? Moving? No, impossible. Just tricks of the light, of a mind frayed by monotony. But what if... No. Focus. Routine. Safety in the known, in the repeated.*

The shower lasted exactly four minutes, a scalding baptism that did little to wash away the dread of the day ahead. As steam filled the small space, Arys closed his eyes, his mind dissecting the day ahead with surgical precision.

*Eighty calls. Eighty masks to don. Eighty times to utter the cursed mantra: "Thank you for calling Spectrum, my name is Arys, how can I help you?" The words echo in my mind, each repetition a nail in the coffin of my individuality. How many times can a man die in one day? Eighty, apparently. And then he wakes up to do it all over again.*

After dressing in a crisp white shirt that seemed to leach what little color remained in his skin and dark slacks that felt like shackles around his legs, Arys moved to the kitchen. The coffee maker gurgled to life, a mechanical heart pumping out liquid vitality.

While it worked, he attended to the only task that offered a glimmer of something other than resigned despair. The spare room, a sanctuary of sorts, housed rows of terrariums lining the walls, reliquaries of some obscure, reptilian faith. The soft glow of heat lamps cast an amber pall, illuminating scales and exoskeletons.

"Good morning," Arys murmured, his voice softer than it would be all day. He moved from tank to tank with reverent care, checking water levels and temperatures, looking for signs of distress. The tarantulas remained motionless, legs curled beneath them in eternal self-embrace. The leopard gecko flicked its tongue, tasting the air with indifferent curiosity. In the largest tank, a ball python uncoiled with sinuous grace, its eyes fixing on Arys with what he desperately wanted to believe was recognition.

*These creatures, in their beautiful simplicity, ask nothing of me but the essentials. They don't demand explanations about the inexplicable, don't complain about the vagaries of existence. In their presence, the anxious knot in my chest loosens, if only for a moment. Is this what peace feels like? Or just a different flavor of isolation?*

The coffee maker beeped, dragging Arys back to reality. He poured the steaming liquid into a travel mug, no cream, no sugar. Bitterness to sharpen focus, another small pain to keep him tethered to the waking world.

At 6:00 AM, Arys locked the apartment door behind him, the click of the deadbolt sounding like the closing of a cell. The city was stirring, a beast slowly waking from uneasy slumber. Early risers hurried past, eyes glazed and fixed forward, clutching travel mugs like talismans against the encroaching day.

*Look at them all, fellow prisoners of routine. Do they feel it too? The crushing weight of sameness, day after day? Or am I alone in this private hell, cursed with awareness in a world of blissful automatons?*

His sedan started on the first try, a small mercy in a world increasingly bereft of them. As he pulled out of the lot and merged into the steadily increasing flow of traffic, Arys kept the radio off, preferring the relative quiet to mentally rehearse opening lines and steel himself for the onslaught of voices that would soon fill his ears.

The sky transformed as he drove, from inky black to deep blue to sickly lavender. Arys watched the metamorphosis with detached interest, his mind already at his desk, already fielding calls, already counting down the seconds until he could return to the sanctuary of his apartment and his pets. The horizon bled red, as if the day itself was wounded before it had even truly begun.

*Another day dawns, bleeding across the sky. Is it a portent? A warning? Or just the indifferent rotation of a planet that cares nothing for the insignificant lives scurrying upon its surface? The sun rises, we toil, it sets. Repeat ad nauseam. Until what? Until when? Is there an end to this, or just an endless cycle of meaningless days?*

The call center loomed ahead, a nondescript building that managed to look both mundane and deeply sinister. Arys pulled into his usual parking spot at 7:25 AM, five minutes before his shift was set to begin. He took a final swig of coffee, the bitter dregs coating his tongue like ash.

Grabbing his ID badge, a technoplastic collar marking him as just another cog in the great machine, Arys stepped out of the car. He squared his shoulders and walked toward the building, unaware that today, finally, the darkness that had long dwelled beneath his skin would begin to seep out, staining the world around him in shades of shadow and possibility.

*The routine is about to be shattered, and in its breaking, something new and terrible will be born. I can feel it, a pressure building behind my eyes, a whisper in the back of my mind. Change is coming. But will it be salvation or damnation?*

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.




## Part 2: The Descent Begins

The automatic doors parted with a hiss, exhaling stale air redolent with desperation and cheap coffee. Arys stepped inside, feeling the building swallow him whole. Fluorescent lights buzzed overhead, casting their sickly pallor over everything and everyone. Faces turned briefly, wan and drawn, before snapping back to screens as if afraid to catch some contagion of discontent.

*Gray partitions stretch out before me, a labyrinth designed not to confound, but to crush. Each step heavier than the last, dragging me deeper into this pit of corporate despair. How many souls have withered here, trapped in the maze of monotony?*

Arys moved through the maze of cubicles, nodding curtly to colleagues whose features seemed as unremarkable and interchangeable as the fabric walls. In this place, individuality was a luxury none could afford.

His workspace was a study in corporate minimalism. A company-issued computer sat on the desk, its screen a blank void waiting to be filled with complaints and queries. The headset lay next to it, an umbilical cord that would soon connect him to the outside world, feeding him a steady diet of frustration and ire.

*Another day, another battle against the void. Will today be the day I finally crack? Or just another in an endless series of indistinguishable torments? The headset stares at me, a plastic parasite waiting to latch onto my brain and suck out whatever humanity remains.*

A single framed photo stood on the desk, depicting a Brazilian salmon pink tarantula. Its eight eyes stared out, unblinking and unjudging. Sometimes Arys wondered if it saw more than he did, despite being trapped in frozen time.

He settled into the chair, its creak a mournful song of plastic and metal. 7:29 AM. One minute of sanctuary remained. Arys closed his eyes, took a deep breath, and centered himself. His face settled into a mask of calm professionalism, a facade as fragile as it was necessary.

*Sixty seconds of freedom. What a joke. Even now, I can feel the tendrils of work wrapping around my mind, squeezing out any thoughts not related to call times and customer satisfaction. But beneath it all, something else stirs. A darkness, a pressure building behind my eyes. What is it? What's changing?*

7:30 AM. The headset chirped. Arys' finger moved to accept the call, muscle memory taking over. For a fraction of a second, he hesitated.

*A part of me screams to reject, to rip off the headset, to run. But the moment passes, unacknowledged. The routine demands to be fed. And I, its willing slave, obey.*

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?" His voice was smooth, practiced, devoid of the maelstrom churning in his chest. It was the voice of a man who had long ago learned to divorce words from thoughts, actions from desires.

"Yeah, hi," a gruff voice responded, frustration already palpable. "My internet's out. Again. Third time this month."

Arys' fingers flew over the keyboard, muscle memory pulling up customer information. Robert Jennings, 1255 Maple Street. Third outage report in 30 days.

*A small part of my mind notes the symmetry – three outages, three decades of life wasted in pursuit of... what, exactly? The American Dream? What a cosmic joke. But something's different today. The customer's frustration... I can almost taste it, a tangible thing seeping through the phone lines.*

"I understand how frustrating that must be, Mr. Jennings. I'd be happy to help you resolve this issue. Can you tell me when you first noticed the problem?" The words flowed automatically, a script etched into his very being.

As the customer launched into a lengthy diatribe about unreliability, Arys felt a strange sensation building in his chest. The shadow within was growing, feeding on sharp words and accusations. His veins darkened, standing out starkly against his pale skin, writhing beneath the surface like agitated serpents.

*What is this? The man's anger... it's flowing into me, filling me up. It should be draining, but instead, I feel... energized. Powerful. Like I could reach through the phone and touch his very essence.*

"Mr. Jennings," Arys interrupted, his voice taking on an unusual timbre that sent a chill down his own spine. "I understand you're upset. But has it occurred to you that perhaps the problem isn't with our service?"

The words slipped out, smooth and dark as oil. There was a moment of silence on the other end, a vacuum pulling at Arys' very essence.

*Did I just say that? Where did that come from? I should be panicking, but instead, I feel... exhilarated. Like I'm finally awake after years of sleepwalking.*

He blinked, shocked at his own audacity. Before he could backpedal, Mr. Jennings spoke again, his tone suddenly subdued, almost entranced.

"I... well, I suppose I haven't considered that. My grandson was fiddling with the TV last weekend. Do you think he might have changed something?"

The shadow in Arys' chest pulsed, a surge of dark satisfaction flooding through him. He could almost taste Mr. Jennings' confusion, his sudden doubt. It was intoxicating.

*This is... incredible. I can feel his emotions shifting, bending to my will. Is this what true power feels like? To shape reality with mere words?*

"That's certainly possible, Mr. Jennings. Why don't we start by checking your TV settings?" Arys guided the now-compliant customer through a series of checks, his voice smooth and compelling.

The call ended with a grateful Mr. Jennings and a resolution that didn't require a technician visit. Arys ended the call and stared at his hands, flexing his fingers as if to make sure they were still under his control. His skin was paler than ever, the black veins more pronounced, pulsing with a life of their own.

*What just happened? That wasn't normal customer service. That was... something else. Something powerful. Something dark. And God help me, I want more.*

"Tough one, huh?"

Arys started at the voice, looking up to see Lila leaning against his cubicle wall. Her dark eyes were warm with sympathy, a stark contrast to the cool blues and grays of the office. For a moment, just a moment, Arys thought he saw something else in her gaze. Fear? Recognition?

*Does she know? Can she see the change in me? The shadows writhing beneath my skin, hungry for more?*

"You could say that," he muttered, glancing at the clock. 12:30 PM. Break time. The numbers swam before his eyes, rearranging into incomprehensible patterns.

As Arys stood to follow Lila to the break room, he felt different. The world around him seemed to have lost a bit more color, the edges of reality blurring and shifting. But within him, the darkness pulsed, alive and hungry. The routine was cracking, and through those cracks, something new and terrifying was beginning to emerge.

*The day is only half over, but everything has changed. What other revelations await? What shadows lurk, ready to be unleashed? For the first time in years, I'm eager to find out.*

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again. But this time, the shadows answer, hungry for what the afternoon may bring.

## Part 3: The Midday Slump

Hours ticked by, calls blending into a nightmarish kaleidoscope. Arys' responses became autonomic, his brain operating on two tracks: one handling calls with practiced efficiency, the other screaming in silent, primal anguish.

The clock crawled towards noon. A familiar midday fatigue set in. Arys' head throbbed, pressure building behind his eyes, less like a headache and more like something clawing to escape. The voices grew more grating, more demanding, words burrowing into his brain like parasites.

*Is this what madness feels like? A slow erosion of self, each call chipping away another piece of my soul. But beneath the fatigue, something else grows. A hunger, vast and insatiable. It terrifies me. It exhilarates me.*

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?" The words fell from his lips, their thousandth repetition eroding his sense of self a little more.

"It's about time someone answered!" A shrill voice pierced the headset like a needle. "I've been on hold for twenty minutes!"

Arys glanced at the call timer. Eight minutes, thirteen seconds. He swallowed the urge to correct her, feeling the lie settle in his stomach like a cold, heavy stone. 

*Another day, another entitled voice screeching into my ear. But today... today it feels different. Her anger, her frustration - I can almost see it, tendrils of dark emotion reaching through the phone line. And God help me, I want to grasp them, to pull them into myself.*

"I apologize for the wait, ma'am. I'd be happy to assist you. May I have your name and account number, please?"

"Margaret Holloway, account number 78291-054. And you'd better do more than just 'assist' me, young man. I want answers!"

Arys' fingers flew over the keyboard, pulling up the account information. Multiple calls in the past week, each marked 'resolved' by the previous representative. He suppressed a sigh that felt more like a sob clawing its way out of his throat.

"I understand your frustration, Mrs. Holloway. Can you tell me what seems to be the problem?"

A fifteen-minute tirade followed. Channels disappearing, remotes not working, picture quality "fuzzier than a peach." Arys listened, interjecting at appropriate intervals with sympathetic noises and clarifying questions. But with each passing moment, he felt something shifting inside, darkness growing, pulsing in time with the complaints.

*Her words... they're not just sounds anymore. They're tangible things, dark and writhing. I can feel them trying to burrow into me, to drag me down. But instead of drowning, I'm... absorbing them? The frustration, the anger - it's becoming a part of me, feeding something I never knew was there.*

As Margaret ranted, a strange sensation built in Arys' chest. The shadow was growing, feeding on sharp words and accusations. His veins darkened, standing out starkly against his pale skin, writhing beneath the surface like agitated serpents.

"Mrs. Holloway," Arys interrupted, his voice taking on an unusual timbre that sent a chill down his own spine. "I understand you're upset. But has it occurred to you that perhaps the problem isn't with our service?"

The words slipped out, smooth and dark as oil. There was a moment of silence on the other end, a vacuum pulling at Arys' very essence.

*What am I doing? This isn't protocol. This isn't me. And yet... it feels right. Like I'm finally speaking my truth, finally exerting some control over this endless cycle of complaints and platitudes.*

He blinked, shocked at his own audacity. Before he could backpedal, Margaret spoke again, her tone suddenly subdued, almost entranced.

"I... well, I suppose I haven't considered that. My grandson was fiddling with the TV last weekend. Do you think he might have changed something?"

The shadow in Arys' chest pulsed, a surge of dark satisfaction flooding through him. He could almost taste Mrs. Holloway's confusion, her sudden doubt. It was intoxicating.

*This power... it's incredible. I can feel her emotions shifting, bending to my will. Is this what I've been missing all along? The ability to shape reality with mere words, to turn the tide of human emotion?*

"That's... that's certainly possible, Mrs. Holloway. Why don't we start by checking your TV settings?"

The rest of the call proceeded smoothly, ending with a grateful Margaret and a resolution that didn't require a technician visit. Arys ended the call and stared at his hands, flexing his fingers as if to make sure they were still under his control. His skin was paler than ever, the black veins more pronounced, pulsing with a life of their own.

*What is happening to me? This isn't normal. This isn't human. And yet... I've never felt more alive, more in control. The darkness inside me, it's not just a metaphor anymore. It's real, and it's hungry.*

"Tough one, huh?"

Arys started at the voice, looking up to see Lila leaning against his cubicle wall. Her dark eyes were warm with sympathy, a stark contrast to the cool blues and grays of the office. For a moment, just a moment, Arys thought he saw something else in her gaze. Fear? Recognition?

"You could say that," he muttered, glancing at the clock. 12:30 PM. Break time. The numbers swam before his eyes, rearranging into incomprehensible patterns.

Lila fell into step beside him as they made their way to the break room. "You handled it well, though. I thought Mrs. Holloway was going to burst a blood vessel, but you calmed her right down."

Arys frowned, the memory of that strange moment nagging at him. "I'm not sure what happened, to be honest. It was... odd."

*Odd doesn't begin to cover it. It felt... right. Powerful. Like I was finally in control, not just a punching bag for angry customers. But how can I explain that without sounding insane?*

Lila bumped his shoulder playfully, but Arys could have sworn he felt her shudder slightly at the contact. "Don't overthink it. Sometimes customers just need a firm hand. Or voice, in our case."

They reached the break room, its stark fluorescent lighting a match for the call floor. Arys sank into a chair and unwrapped the sandwich he had prepared that morning. Each bite was mechanical, fuel for the afternoon ahead. But with every swallow, he felt the darkness inside growing, feeding not on food, but on something else. Something intangible yet vital.

Lila chatted about office gossip, her voice a soothing backdrop to Arys' whirling thoughts. He nodded at appropriate intervals, but his mind was elsewhere, replaying the call with Margaret, trying to understand the shadow he had felt growing inside.

*What is happening to me? This power, this darkness... it should terrify me. But instead, I feel... excited. Alive. For the first time in years, I'm not just going through the motions. I'm changing, evolving. But into what?*

All too soon, their fifteen minutes were up. Lila stood, stretching. "Back to the grind. You good?"

Arys nodded, forcing a smile that didn't reach his eyes. "Always." The lie tasted bitter on his tongue, but beneath it was a hint of something else. Something dark and sweet and terrifying.

They walked back to their desks, Arys feeling the weight of the afternoon settling onto his shoulders. The shadow inside stirred restlessly, as if awakened by the brief respite. It whispered promises of power and release from the monotony that had defined his existence for so long.

Arys sat down, adjusted his headset, and took a deep breath. The phone chirped, the sound no longer merely annoying but actively painful, like a spike being driven into his brain.

*Here we go again. But this time... this time it's different. I'm different. The shadows are awake now, hungry. And I am their vessel, willing or not.*

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again. The cycle continued, but something had shifted. A crack had appeared in the routine, and through it, something dark and unfamiliar seeped. With each call, each complaint, each resolution, Arys felt himself changing. The world around him seemed to darken, shadows lengthening and deepening even under the harsh fluorescent lights.

*And deep within, in the darkest recesses of my mind, something ancient and terrible stirs. It has tasted freedom, tasted power, and it hungers for more. The afternoon stretches before me like an abyss, and I find myself both terrified and exhilarated by what might emerge from its depths.*

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. The shadows hunger, and I am their vessel, willing or not.

## Part 4: The Unraveling

The afternoon dragged on, a parade of voices and problems blurring together. Arys felt stretched thin, his skin pulled taut over what felt like a festering wound. The shadow inside pulsed with each call, growing incrementally with every "How can I help you today?" It fed on frustration, anger, despair seeping through the phone lines, gorging itself on negative emotions until Arys felt bloated with darkness.

*I'm drowning in a sea of other people's misery, but instead of pulling me under, it's... feeding me. Every complaint, every burst of anger - it's fuel for this thing growing inside me. What am I becoming?*

4:17 PM. Forty-three minutes left in his shift. The headset chirped once more. Arys took a deep breath, centering himself before answering. The air felt thick, as if reality itself was congealing around him.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?" The words fell from his lips like drops of black ichor, each syllable leaving a stain on the world.

"Hello," a soft, tremulous voice responded. "I... I'm not sure you can help me. I don't even know if this is the right number to call."

Arys frowned, the scripted response dying on his lips. Something in the caller's voice – fear, perhaps, or deep distress – cut through his fatigue like a knife, carving a path straight to the writhing darkness within.

*This voice... it's different. Raw. Vulnerable. I can almost see the fear radiating through the phone line, tendrils of icy blue terror reaching out to me. And God help me, I want to grasp them, to pull them into myself.*

"I'm here to assist you with whatever you need," he said, his tone gentler than usual, a spider's silk invitation. "Can you tell me what's going on?"

"It's my TV," the woman said, her voice barely above a whisper. "There's... there's something wrong with it. I know how that sounds, but... I'm scared."

Arys' frown deepened, furrows in his brow like canyons on a deathly pale landscape. "Can you elaborate on what you mean by 'wrong,' ma'am?"

"It's showing things. Terrible things. Things that haven't happened yet, but... but I think they will." The woman's voice cracked. "I saw my son. He was... oh God, there was so much blood."

A chill ran down Arys' spine, a glacier of dread carving its way through his being. This was far outside the realm of typical customer service. He should transfer the call, escalate to a supervisor. Instead, he found himself leaning closer to the screen, as if he could reach through to the distressed woman on the other end.

*This isn't a normal call. This is... something else. Something beyond broken cable boxes and billing disputes. I should end this, pass it along. But the darkness inside me... it's resonating with her fear, hungry for it. And I'm powerless to resist.*

"Ma'am, I want you to take a deep breath. Can you do that for me?" 

He heard a shaky inhale, then exhale. 

"Good," Arys continued, his voice taking on a resonance that seemed to vibrate in the air around him. "Now, I need you to listen to me very carefully. What you're seeing isn't real. It's not the future. It's just... shadows. Reflections of fear, nothing more."

As he spoke, Arys felt the familiar darkness stirring inside him. But this time, instead of feeding on negativity, it seemed to be reaching out, extending through the phone line towards the frightened woman. He could almost see it, a tendril of shadow stretching across miles, seeking her out.

*What am I doing? This isn't protocol. This isn't even human. I'm reaching out with... something. Power. Darkness. Comfort? I don't understand it, but I can't stop it. Don't want to stop it.*

"How... how can you be sure?" she asked, desperate hope in her voice.

Arys closed his eyes, concentrating on the shadow within. The darkness behind his eyelids was alive, swirling with eldritch patterns. "Because I can see them too," he said, the words coming from someplace deep and unknown. "The shadows, the fears. They're loud and they're frightening, but they're not real. Not unless we make them real."

*I feel something happening, a connection forming between myself and the caller. The shadow extends, touches her, and suddenly I can *see*. Not with my eyes, but with some other sense. I see the woman's fear as a dark cloud around her, see malevolent images flickering on her TV screen. Scenes of violence and horror play out in rapid succession, each more terrible than the last.*

And Arys saw how to dispel them.

"I want you to walk to your TV," he said, his voice taking on a strange, compelling quality. It seemed to reverberate through the office, causing nearby coworkers to shiver involuntarily. "Don't look at the screen. Just reach out and place your hand on it."

He heard movement, then a soft gasp. "It's... it's warm. Like it's alive."

"That's right," Arys said, though he had no idea how he knew this. His own skin felt feverish, black veins pulsing visibly now. "Now, imagine all those frightening images being drawn into your hand. They're just shadows, and shadows can't exist in the light. You're going to pull them out of the TV and into yourself, where they'll dissolve like mist in the sun."

*This is insane. Impossible. And yet... I can feel it happening. The shadows, the fear - they're flowing through me, from her to me. I'm a conduit for something vast and terrible and wonderful.*

As he spoke, Arys felt the shadow inside him pulse and grow. It wasn't painful, but intense, like a muscle being used for the first time. He could feel the woman's fear, taste her terror on his tongue. It was intoxicating.

"I... I think it's working," the woman said, awe replacing fear in her voice. "The images... they're fading. They're coming into me, but... they're not scary anymore. They're just... nothing."

Arys exhaled slowly, feeling the connection between them start to dissipate. The shadows drawn from her TV seemed to flow into him, adding to the growing darkness within. "Good. That's very good. How do you feel now?"

"I feel... lighter. Like I've put down a heavy weight I didn't know I was carrying." There was a pause, then, "Thank you. I don't understand what just happened, but... thank you."

"You're welcome," Arys said, suddenly feeling exhausted yet somehow more alive than he'd ever been. "Is there anything else I can help you with today?"

"No, I... I think I'm okay now. Thank you again."

The call ended. Arys slumped in his chair, drained in a way he'd never experienced before. He looked at his hands, half-expecting to see shadow leaking from his fingertips. But they looked normal – pale skin, black veins.

*What just happened? What did I do? I helped her, I know that. But how? And at what cost? The darkness inside me... it's grown. Changed. I've changed.*

Only... the veins were darker than before. More pronounced. They seemed to shift under his gaze, as if trying to form patterns just beyond comprehension.

Movement caught his eye. Arys looked up to see Lila staring at him from her cubicle, her eyes wide with a mixture of concern and... was that fear?

"Arys," she said, her voice hushed, trembling. "What did you just do?"

Before he could answer, a sharp pain lanced through his head. The fluorescent lights suddenly seemed too bright, their buzzing morphing into a hellish chorus of screams. The world around him began to distort, shadows stretching and twisting into impossible shapes.

*Something's wrong. The power I've been channeling all day... it's too much. I can't contain it. The world is breaking apart around me, and I'm the epicenter.*

The faces of his coworkers elongated, their features melting like wax under infernal heat.

*The last thing I see before darkness claims me is Lila rushing towards me, her mouth open in a shout I can't hear. But behind her, rising up like a tidal wave of night, is a wall of shadow, poised to engulf us all.*

*Then, nothing but shadows. But in those shadows, something waits. Something old. Something hungry. And it has my face.*

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. But this time, the shadows answer.

## Part 5: The Awakening

Consciousness returned slowly, like a tide of black ink creeping up a shore of bleached bone. The first thing Arys became aware of was smell. Antiseptic, sterile, undercut by something else. Something organic, wrong. It wasn't the stale coffee and desperation of the call center, but it wasn't entirely unfamiliar either.

*Where am I? What happened? The last thing I remember is... shadows. Darkness. Power beyond imagining. Did I dream it all?*

Arys blinked. His vision swam into focus. The world seemed overexposed, too bright, too sharp. White ceiling. Beeping machines. A hospital room, viewed through a lens of unreality.

"Welcome back to the land of the living."

Arys turned his head, wincing at the movement. The simple action felt like tearing through a membrane of reality, gossamer strands of what-should-be clinging to his skin. Lila sat beside the bed, dark circles under her eyes suggesting a long vigil. But there was something else in her gaze – wariness, tension in her posture speaking of prey in the presence of a predator.

*Lila. She was there. She saw... what exactly? What does she know? What do I know?*

"What happened?" Arys croaked, his throat dry as a tomb.

Lila leaned forward, offering a cup of water. As she moved, Arys could have sworn he saw her shadow detach slightly, trailing behind her movements like a reluctant pet. "You collapsed. Right after that weird call. You've been out for a couple of hours."

Arys sipped the water, fragments of memory flashing through his mind. The frightened woman. Shadows. An inexplicable connection. Each recollection was a shard of dark glass, cutting into his psyche as it fell into place.

*Hours? It feels like I've been gone for centuries. Like I've traveled to the edge of the universe and back. And that call... it was real. All of it was real.*

"The doctors couldn't find anything wrong," Lila continued, her voice forced casual, not matching the tightness around her eyes. "They're chalking it up to stress and exhaustion."

*If only it were that simple. They have no idea what's awakened inside me. What I've become. Do I even know?*

"I'm sorry for causing trouble," Arys said, the words feeling hollow, inadequate.

Lila waved off the apology, the motion leaving a brief afterimage in the air. "Please. You gave us the most excitement we've had in months. Though..." She hesitated, biting her lip. "Arys, what happened on that call? I've never seen anything like it. It was as if... as if you were glowing. But dark, if that makes any sense."

Arys closed his eyes, suddenly very tired. The darkness behind his eyelids was alive, swirling with patterns almost comprehensible, always just out of reach. "I don't know, Lila. I really don't know."

*I'm lying. I do know. Something impossible happened. Something wonderful and terrible. But how can I explain it when I barely understand it myself?*

A knock at the door saved him from further questioning. A doctor entered, clipboard in hand. "Mr. Zellinger? Good to see you awake. How are you feeling?"

The next half hour was a blur of tests and questions. In the end, as Lila had said, nothing was concretely wrong. Stress, exhaustion, possibly a migraine. Arys nodded along, not bothering to explain that it felt like something fundamental had shifted inside him, that the world no longer seemed quite real – or that reality had expanded to include things that shouldn't be.

*They have no idea. How could they? Their instruments can't measure what's happened to me. Their science can't explain the shadows that now course through my veins.*

At 7:30 PM, Arys stepped out of a taxi in front of his apartment building. Lila had offered to drive, but he had insisted on going alone. He needed time to think, to process, to try and understand the new awareness thrumming through his veins like living shadow.

The familiar walls of his apartment greeted him, unchanged despite the seismic shift in his world. Yet even here, in his sanctuary, things seemed... off. Shadows pooled in corners where they shouldn't, defying laws of light and space.

*Home. But is it still home? Can anywhere be home when you've touched the void and it's touched you back?*

Arys moved through his evening routine on autopilot – hanging his jacket, removing his shoes, washing his hands. Each action felt like a performance, a ritual enacted to maintain the illusion of normalcy.

In the bathroom, Arys paused, staring at his reflection. His skin seemed paler than ever, almost translucent, the black veins more pronounced. As he watched, he could have sworn he saw them pulse, like living things beneath his skin.

*What am I becoming? The man in the mirror... is he still me? Or something else wearing my skin?*

For a moment, just a moment, Arys thought he saw his reflection smile – a dark, hungry expression that sent a shiver of both fear and exhilaration down his spine.

Shaking his head, Arys made his way to the spare room. The soft glow of heat lamps welcomed him, along with the quiet rustling of his pets. Here, among his scaled and chitinous friends, he felt some tension leave his body. Yet even this familiar space felt changed, charged with a new energy he couldn't quite name.

"Hey there," Arys murmured, approaching the ball python's enclosure. The snake raised its head, tongue flicking out to taste the air. Was it his imagination, or did the python's eyes seem to glow with an inner light, reflecting the newfound darkness within him?

*Even they can sense it. The change in me. Are they afraid? Or... excited?*

Arys opened the tank and gently lifted the python. The cool, smooth scales against his skin were comforting, grounding. Yet as he held the snake, he felt a strange connection forming, as if the darkness within him was reaching out, communing with the creature's primordial nature.

*As I cradle the python, my mind wanders back to the events of the day. The strange call. Shadows seen, felt, manipulated. It should be terrifying, but instead, I feel... curious. Excited, even. For the first time in years, tomorrow holds the promise of the unknown, of power waiting to be explored and mastered.*

After ensuring all his pets were fed and comfortable, each interaction charged with a new, unspoken understanding, Arys retreated to his bedroom. He sat on the edge of the bed, staring at his alarm clock. 9:45 PM. In his normal routine, he would be asleep in fifteen minutes, ready to wake at 5:30 AM and do it all over again.

But nothing felt normal anymore. The shadows in the room seemed to lean in, waiting, expectant.

*The routine. My anchor for so long. But now... now it feels like a chain. A limitation. There's so much more out there, so much to explore. How can I go back to that life after what I've experienced?*

Arys reached out, his finger hovering over the alarm switch. The events of the day flashed through his mind – the monotony of the morning, the strange incident with Mrs. Holloway, the intense connection with the frightened caller, his collapse. Each memory was tinged with darkness, with a power that both thrilled and terrified him.

*Something has awakened inside me. Something dark, ancient, utterly fascinating. The world has changed, or perhaps I have changed, opening my eyes to a reality that has always existed just beyond the veil of the mundane.*

His finger touched the switch. Flicked it off.

For the first time in three years, Arys went to bed without setting an alarm. Tomorrow would come with or without the blaring 5:30 AM wake-up call. And for once, he was eager to see what it would bring, what new shadows would reveal themselves in the light of day.

As Arys drifted off to sleep, he could have sworn he felt the shadows in the room reaching out, caressing him like old friends welcoming him home. In his dreams, he stood at the edge of a vast, dark ocean, waves of shadow lapping at his feet. The darkness called to him, promising power, knowledge, terrible and wonderful transformation.

And somewhere in the distance, a voice that was both his and not his called his name, beckoning him deeper into the abyss.

*Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. But this time, as consciousness fades, it's the shadows that answer, hungry for what tomorrow may bring.*
