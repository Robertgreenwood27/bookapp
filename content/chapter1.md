## Part 1: The Morning Ritual

The alarm's shrill cry lacerates the darkness. 5:30 AM. Another day begins. My eyes snap open, bloodshot and burning, hand already moving to silence the noise. Lie still. Breathe. Feel the weight of existence settling onto my chest, a familiar, unwelcome guest.

Thoughts cascade through my mind, shards of glass cutting through the fog of sleep. Each one a reminder of the monotony ahead, the endless cycle of calls and complaints. Eighty voices waiting to chip away at my sanity. Eighty opportunities for something to crack, for the darkness lurking beneath my skin to seep out.

A soft groan escapes me, born from the marrow of my bones. Swing legs over the side of the bed. Floorboards creak, a skeletal xylophone playing a dirge for another day begun. Move through the darkness, muscle memory guiding me through this prison cell I call home.

Bathroom mirror. No comfort here, only stark reality. Disheveled dark hair frames a face etched with discontent. But it's my skin that draws my gaze, pale as a corpse and shot through with black veins. They writhe beneath the surface, a network of dark rivers carrying something far more insidious than blood.

Shower. Four minutes of scalding baptism. Steam fills the small space as I close my eyes, mind dissecting the day ahead with surgical precision. Eighty calls. Eighty masks to don. Eighty times to utter the cursed mantra: "Thank you for calling Spectrum, my name is Arys, how can I help you?" The words echo in my mind, each repetition a nail in the coffin of my individuality.

Dress. Crisp white shirt leaching what little color remains in my skin. Dark slacks, shackles around my legs. Move to the kitchen, coffee maker gurgling to life, a mechanical heart pumping out liquid vitality.

While it works, attend to the only task that offers a glimmer of something other than resigned despair. The spare room, a sanctuary of sorts. Rows of terrariums line the walls, reliquaries of some obscure, reptilian faith. The soft glow of heat lamps casts an amber pall, illuminating scales and exoskeletons.

"Good morning," I murmur, voice softer than it will be all day. Move from tank to tank with reverent care. Check water levels, temperatures, look for signs of distress. The tarantulas remain motionless, legs curled beneath them in eternal self-embrace. The leopard gecko flicks its tongue, tasting the air with indifferent curiosity. In the largest tank, a ball python uncoils with sinuous grace, eyes fixing on me with what I desperately want to believe is recognition.

These creatures, in their beautiful simplicity, ask nothing of me but the essentials. They don't demand explanations about the inexplicable, don't complain about the vagaries of existence. In their presence, the anxious knot in my chest loosens, if only for a moment. But even this respite is fleeting, the ticking clock a constant reminder of the world that waits beyond this room, hungry and impatient.

Coffee maker beeps, dragging me back to reality. Pour steaming liquid into a travel mug, no cream, no sugar. Bitterness to sharpen focus, another small pain to keep me tethered to the waking world.

6:00 AM. Lock the apartment door behind me, the click of the deadbolt sounding like the closing of a cell. The city stirs, a beast slowly waking from uneasy slumber. Early risers hurry past, eyes glazed and fixed forward, clutching travel mugs like talismans against the encroaching day.

My sedan starts on the first try, a small mercy in a world increasingly bereft of them. Pull out of the lot, merge into the steadily increasing flow of traffic. Metal veins carrying the lifeblood of commerce and tedium. Radio off, preferring the relative quiet to mentally rehearse opening lines, to steel myself for the onslaught of voices that will soon fill my ears.

The sky transforms as I drive, inky black to deep blue to sickly lavender. Watch the metamorphosis with detached interest, mind already at my desk, already fielding calls, already counting down the seconds until I can return to the sanctuary of my apartment and my pets. The horizon bleeds red, as if the day itself is wounded before it has even truly begun.

The call center looms ahead, a nondescript building that manages to look both mundane and deeply sinister. It squats on the landscape like a bloated tick, gorged on the life force of those trapped within its walls. Pull into my usual parking spot at 7:25 AM, five minutes before my shift begins. Take a final swig of coffee, bitter dregs coating my tongue like ash.

Grab the ID badge, a technoplastic collar marking me as just another cog in the great machine. Step out of the car. Another day beginning. Another series of identical hours stretched before me, a purgatory of fluorescent lights and forced politeness. Another opportunity for something to go terribly, unexpectedly wrong.

Square my shoulders and walk toward the building, unaware that today, finally, the darkness that has long dwelled beneath my skin will begin to seep out, staining the world around me in shades of shadow and possibility. The routine is about to be shattered, and in its breaking, something new and terrible will be born.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.

## Part 2: The Descent Begins

Automatic doors part with a hiss, exhaling stale air redolent with desperation and cheap coffee. Step inside, feel the building swallow me whole. Fluorescent lights buzz overhead, angry insects casting their sickly pallor over everything and everyone. Faces turn, wan and drawn, snapping back to screens as if afraid to catch some contagion of discontent.

Move through the maze of cubicles, each step heavier than the last. Gray partitions stretch out before me, a labyrinth designed not to confound, but to crush. Nod curtly to colleagues, their features as unremarkable and interchangeable as the fabric walls. In this place, individuality is a luxury none can afford.

My workspace, a study in corporate minimalism. Company-issued computer, screen a blank void waiting to be filled with complaints and queries. Headset, an umbilical cord connecting me to the outside world, feeding me a steady diet of frustration and ire. Small notepad, pages blank save for a few scribbled reminders, each a tally mark counting down to... what? Freedom? Oblivion?

Single framed photo, a Brazilian salmon pink tarantula. Eight eyes stare out, unblinking, unjudging. Sometimes I wonder if it sees more than I do, despite being trapped in frozen time.

Settle into the chair, its creak a mournful song of plastic and metal. 7:29 AM. One minute of sanctuary remains. Close eyes, deep breath, center myself. Face settles into a mask of calm professionalism, facade as fragile as it is necessary.

7:30 AM. Headset chirps. Finger moves to accept the call, muscle memory taking over. For a fraction of a second, hesitate. A part of me screams to reject, to rip off the headset, to run. But the moment passes, unacknowledged. Answer.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?" Voice smooth, practiced, devoid of the maelstrom churning in my chest. The voice of a man who has long ago learned to divorce words from thoughts, actions from desires.

"Yeah, hi," a gruff voice responds, frustration already palpable. "My internet's out. Again. Third time this month."

Fingers fly over the keyboard, muscle memory pulling up customer information. Robert Jennings, 1255 Maple Street. Third outage report in 30 days. A small part of my mind notes the symmetry – three outages, three decades of life wasted in pursuit of... what, exactly?

"I understand how frustrating that must be, Mr. Jennings. I'd be happy to help you resolve this issue. Can you tell me when you first noticed the problem?" The words flow automatically, a script etched into my very being.

Customer launches into a lengthy diatribe about unreliability. Feel the familiar detachment settle over me. Watch myself from a distance, this pale, black-veined creature going through the motions of human interaction. Part of my mind focuses on the call, asking necessary questions, guiding the conversation. Another part observes, cataloging tone, background noises, subtle tells.

And a third part, buried deep, seethes. *This is what you've become,* it whispers, voice a slithering thing in the recesses of consciousness. *A robot in human skin, parroting the same lines day after day. Is this all you're meant for? To be a conduit for the grievances of others?*

Push the thought aside, focus on the task. "I see, Mr. Jennings. I'm going to run a diagnostic on your line. This will just take a moment."

Glance at the photo on my desk. Tarantula's eyes stare back, eight points of stillness in the chaotic world of customer service. For a moment, envy its simplicity of existence. No calls to answer, no masks to wear. Just the pure, uncomplicated drive to survive.

Diagnostic completes with a soft ping. "Mr. Jennings, I've identified an issue with the signal strength to your home. I'm going to reset your connection remotely. You should see your router lights begin to flash in just a moment."

"Well, it's about time," Mr. Jennings grumbles, voice grating against my eardrums. "You know, I've been a customer for fifteen years, and I gotta say, the service ain't what it used to be."

*Fifteen years of the same complaints, the same problems, the same solutions,* I think, a wave of existential nausea washing over me. *Is this all there is? An endless loop of minor inconveniences and petty grievances?* Out loud, I say, "I apologize for the inconvenience, Mr. Jennings. We value your long-standing loyalty. Is there anything else I can assist you with today?"

"No, that's it. Just get it fixed."

"Certainly, sir. Thank you for your patience. Have a great day." End the call, the fake smile dropping from my face like a discarded mask. Feel hollowed out, as if each word spoken had carved away a little more of my essence.

One down. Seventy-nine to go.

Thirty seconds before the next call. Roll shoulders, try to release some of the tension already building. Catch sight of my reflection in the computer screen. For a moment, just a moment, see something shift beneath my skin. Black veins writhe like agitated snakes.

Blink, and the illusion vanishes. But a seed of unease has been planted, a creeping dread that something is fundamentally wrong. Not just with my job, or my life, but with the very fabric of my being.

Headset chirps again, dragging me back to grim reality. Straighten in the chair, professional mask sliding back into place. But beneath it, in the depths of my mind, something dark and ancient stirs. It awakens to the realization that this façade, this life, is nothing more than a brittle shell. And like all shells, it is destined to crack.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?"

The cycle begins anew, but this time, with each word spoken, each second ticked away, the darkness grows. The routine I've clung to for so long is beginning to unravel, thread by agonizing thread. And in its unraveling, something terrifying and wonderful waits to be born.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again. The shadows hunger, and I am their unwitting vessel.

## Part 3: The Midday Slump

Hours tick by, calls blending into a nightmarish kaleidoscope. Responses become autonomic, brain operating on two tracks: one handling calls with practiced efficiency, the other screaming in silent, primal anguish.

Clock crawls towards noon. Familiar midday fatigue sets in. Head throbs, pressure building behind eyes, less like headache, more like something clawing to escape. Voices grow more grating, more demanding, words burrowing into brain like parasites.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?" Words fall from lips, thousandth repetition, each eroding sense of self a little more.

"It's about time someone answered!" Shrill voice pierces headset like a needle. "I've been on hold for twenty minutes!"

Glance at call timer. Eight minutes, thirteen seconds. Swallow the urge to correct, feel the lie settle in stomach like a cold, heavy stone. "I apologize for the wait, ma'am. I'd be happy to assist you. May I have your name and account number, please?"

"Margaret Holloway, account number 78291-054. And you'd better do more than just 'assist' me, young man. I want answers!"

Fingers fly over keyboard, pulling up account information. Multiple calls past week, each marked 'resolved' by previous representative. Suppress a sigh that feels more like a sob clawing its way out of throat.

"I understand your frustration, Mrs. Holloway. Can you tell me what seems to be the problem?"

Fifteen-minute tirade follows. Channels disappearing, remotes not working, picture quality "fuzzier than a peach." Listen, interject at appropriate intervals with sympathetic noises, clarifying questions. But with each passing moment, feel something shifting inside, darkness growing, pulsing in time with complaints.

As Margaret rants, strange sensation builds in chest. Shadow growing, feeding on sharp words and accusations. Veins darken, stand out starkly against pale skin, writhing beneath surface like agitated serpents.

"Mrs. Holloway," interrupt, voice taking on unusual timbre that sends chill down own spine. "I understand you're upset. But has it occurred to you that perhaps the problem isn't with our service?"

Words slip out, smooth and dark as oil. Moment of silence on other end, vacuum pulling at very essence.

Blink, shocked at own audacity. Before backpedaling, Margaret speaks again, tone suddenly subdued, almost entranced.

"I... well, I suppose I haven't considered that. My grandson was fiddling with the TV last weekend. Do you think he might have changed something?"

Shadow in chest pulses, surge of dark satisfaction flooding through. Can almost taste Mrs. Holloway's confusion, sudden doubt. Intoxicating. "That's... that's certainly possible, Mrs. Holloway. Why don't we start by checking your TV settings?"

Rest of call proceeds smoothly, ending with grateful Margaret, resolution without technician visit. End call, stare at hands, flexing fingers as if to make sure still under control. Skin paler than ever, black veins more pronounced, pulsing with life of their own.

"Tough one, huh?"

Start at voice, look up to see Lila leaning against cubicle wall. Dark eyes warm with sympathy, stark contrast to cool blues and grays of office. For a moment, just a moment, think I see something else in her gaze. Fear? Recognition?

"You could say that," mutter, glancing at clock. 12:30 PM. Break time. Numbers swim before eyes, rearranging into incomprehensible patterns.

Lila falls into step beside me as we make way to break room. "You handled it well, though. I thought Mrs. Holloway was going to burst a blood vessel, but you calmed her right down."

Frown, memory of strange moment nagging. "I'm not sure what happened, to be honest. It was... odd." *Odd doesn't begin to cover it*, think. *It felt... right. Powerful.*

Lila bumps shoulder playfully, but swear I feel her shudder slightly at contact. "Don't overthink it. Sometimes customers just need a firm hand. Or voice, in our case."

Reach break room, stark fluorescent lighting match for call floor. Sink into chair, unwrap sandwich prepared this morning. Each bite mechanical, fuel for afternoon ahead. But with every swallow, feel darkness inside growing, feeding not on food, but something else. Something intangible yet vital.

Lila chats about office gossip, voice soothing backdrop to whirling thoughts. Nod at appropriate intervals, but mind elsewhere, replaying call with Margaret, trying to understand shadow felt growing inside. With each passing moment, world around loses bit more color, edges of reality blurring and shifting.

All too soon, fifteen minutes up. Lila stands, stretching. "Back to the grind. You good?"

Nod, force smile that doesn't reach eyes. "Always." Lie tastes bitter on tongue, but beneath it hint of something else. Something dark and sweet and terrifying.

Walk back to desks, feel weight of afternoon settling onto shoulders. Shadow inside stirs restlessly, as if awakened by brief respite. It whispers promises of power and release from monotony that has defined existence for so long.

Sit down, adjust headset, take deep breath. Phone chirps, sound no longer merely annoying but actively painful, like spike being driven into brain.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?"

Cycle continues, but something has shifted. Crack has appeared in routine, and through it, something dark and unfamiliar seeps. With each call, each complaint, each resolution, feel myself changing. World around seems to darken, shadows lengthening and deepening even under harsh fluorescent lights.

And deep within, in darkest recesses of mind, something ancient and terrible stirs. It has tasted freedom, tasted power, and it hungers for more. Afternoon stretches before me like abyss, and I find myself both terrified and exhilarated by what might emerge from its depths.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. The shadows hunger, and I am their vessel, willing or not.

## Part 4: The Unraveling

Afternoon drags on, parade of voices and problems blurring together. Feel stretched thin, skin pulled taut over festering wound. Shadow inside pulses with each call, growing incrementally with every "How can I help you today?" It feeds on frustration, anger, despair seeping through phone lines, gorging itself on negative emotions until I feel bloated with darkness.

4:17 PM. Forty-three minutes left in shift. Headset chirps once more. Deep breath, centering before answering. Air feels thick, as if reality itself congealing around me.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. Here we go again.

"Thank you for calling Spectrum. My name is Arys. How can I help you today?" Words fall from lips like drops of black ichor, each syllable leaving stain on world.

"Hello," soft, tremulous voice responds. "I... I'm not sure you can help me. I don't even know if this is the right number to call."

Frown, scripted response dying on lips. Something in caller's voice – fear, perhaps, or deep distress – cuts through fatigue like knife, carving path straight to writhing darkness within.

"I'm here to assist you with whatever you need," say, tone gentler than usual, spider's silk invitation. "Can you tell me what's going on?"

"It's my TV," woman says, voice barely above whisper. "There's... there's something wrong with it. I know how that sounds, but... I'm scared."

Frown deepens, furrows in brow like canyons on deathly pale landscape. "Can you elaborate on what you mean by 'wrong,' ma'am?"

"It's showing things. Terrible things. Things that haven't happened yet, but... but I think they will." Woman's voice cracks. "I saw my son. He was... oh God, there was so much blood."

Chill runs down spine, glacier of dread carving way through being. Far outside realm of typical customer service. Should transfer call, escalate to supervisor. Instead, find myself leaning closer to screen, as if could reach through to distressed woman on other end. Shadows in peripheral vision deepen, reach out with tendrils of inky blackness.

"Ma'am, I want you to take a deep breath. Can you do that for me?" 

Hear shaky inhale, exhale. 

"Good," continue, voice taking on resonance that seems to vibrate in air around me. "Now, I need you to listen to me very carefully. What you're seeing isn't real. It's not the future. It's just... shadows. Reflections of fear, nothing more."

As I speak, feel familiar darkness stirring inside. But this time, instead of feeding on negativity, it seems to be reaching out, extending through phone line towards frightened woman. Can almost see it, tendril of shadow stretching across miles, seeking her out.

"How... how can you be sure?" she asks, desperate hope in voice.

Close eyes, concentrate on shadow within. Darkness behind eyelids alive, pulsing with eldritch patterns. "Because I can see them too," say, words coming from someplace deep and unknown. "The shadows, the fears. They're loud and they're frightening, but they're not real. Not unless we make them real."

Feel something happening, connection forming between myself and caller. Shadow extends, touches her, and suddenly I can *see*. Not with eyes, but with some other sense. See woman's fear as dark cloud around her, see malevolent images flickering on TV screen. Scenes of violence and horror play out in rapid succession, each more terrible than last.

And I see how to dispel them.

"I want you to walk to your TV," say, voice taking on strange, compelling quality. It seems to reverberate through office, causing nearby coworkers to shiver involuntarily. "Don't look at the screen. Just reach out and place your hand on it."

Hear movement, then soft gasp. "It's... it's warm. Like it's alive."

"That's right," say, though no idea how I know this. Own skin feels feverish, black veins pulsing visibly now. "Now, imagine all those frightening images being drawn into your hand. They're just shadows, and shadows can't exist in the light. You're going to pull them out of the TV and into yourself, where they'll dissolve like mist in the sun."

As I speak, feel shadow inside pulse and grow. Not painful, but intense, like muscle being used for first time. Can feel woman's fear, taste her terror on tongue. Intoxicating.

"I... I think it's working," woman says, awe replacing fear in voice. "The images... they're fading. They're coming into me, but... they're not scary anymore. They're just... nothing."

Exhale slowly, feeling connection between us start to dissipate. Shadows drawn from her TV seem to flow into me, adding to growing darkness within. "Good. That's very good. How do you feel now?"

"I feel... lighter. Like I've put down a heavy weight I didn't know I was carrying." Pause, then, "Thank you. I don't understand what just happened, but... thank you."

"You're welcome," say, suddenly feeling exhausted yet somehow more alive than ever been. "Is there anything else I can help you with today?"

"No, I... I think I'm okay now. Thank you again."

Call ends. Slump in chair, drained in way never experienced before. Look at hands, half-expecting to see shadow leaking from fingertips. But they look normal – pale skin, black veins.

Only... veins are darker than before. More pronounced. Seem to shift under gaze, as if trying to form patterns just beyond comprehension.

Movement catches eye. Look up to see Lila staring from her cubicle, eyes wide with mixture of concern and... is that fear?

"Arys," she says, voice hushed, trembling. "What did you just do?"

Before can answer, sharp pain lances through head. Fluorescent lights suddenly too bright, buzzing morphing into hellish chorus of screams. World around begins to distort, shadows stretching and twisting into impossible shapes. Faces of coworkers elongate, features melting like wax under infernal heat.

Last thing I see before darkness claims me is Lila rushing towards me, mouth open in shout I can't hear. But behind her, rising up like tidal wave of night, is wall of shadow, poised to engulf us all.

Then, nothing but shadows. But in those shadows, something waits. Something old. Something hungry. And it has my face.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. But this time, the shadows answer.

## Part 5: The Awakening

Consciousness returns slowly, tide of black ink creeping up shore of bleached bone. First awareness: smell. Antiseptic, sterile, undercut by something else. Something organic, wrong. Not stale coffee and desperation of call center, but not entirely unfamiliar.

Blink. Vision swims into focus. World overexposed, too bright, too sharp. White ceiling. Beeping machines. Hospital room, viewed through lens of unreality.

"Welcome back to the land of the living."

Turn head, wince at movement. Simple action feels like tearing through membrane of reality, gossamer strands of what-should-be clinging to skin. Lila sits beside bed, dark circles under eyes suggesting long vigil. But something else in her gaze – wariness, tension in posture speaking of prey in presence of predator.

"What happened?" Croak, throat dry as tomb.

Lila leans forward, offers cup of water. As she moves, swear I see her shadow detach slightly, trailing behind movements like reluctant pet. "You collapsed. Right after that weird call. You've been out for a couple of hours."

Sip water, fragments of memory flashing through mind. Frightened woman. Shadows. Inexplicable connection. Each recollection a shard of dark glass, cutting into psyche as it falls into place.

"The doctors couldn't find anything wrong," Lila continues, voice forced casual, not matching tightness around eyes. "They're chalking it up to stress and exhaustion."

*If only it were that simple*, think. Shadows in corners of room pulse in agreement, reach out with tendrils of darkness before retreating, testing boundaries. Out loud, say, "I'm sorry for causing trouble."

Lila waves off apology, motion leaving brief afterimage in air. "Please. You gave us the most excitement we've had in months. Though..." Hesitates, bites lip. "Arys, what happened on that call? I've never seen anything like it. It was as if... as if you were glowing. But dark, if that makes any sense."

Close eyes, suddenly very tired. Darkness behind eyelids alive, swirling with patterns almost comprehensible, always just out of reach. "I don't know, Lila. I really don't know."

Knock at door saves from further questioning. Doctor enters, clipboard in hand. "Mr. Zellinger? Good to see you awake. How are you feeling?"

Next half hour a blur of tests, questions. In end, as Lila said, nothing concretely wrong. Stress, exhaustion, possibly migraine. Nod along, not bothering to explain that it feels like something fundamental has shifted inside, that world no longer seems quite real – or that reality has expanded to include things that shouldn't be.

7:30 PM. Step out of taxi in front of apartment building. Lila offered to drive, but insisted on going alone. Need time to think, to process, to try and understand new awareness thrumming through veins like living shadow.

Familiar walls of apartment greet me, unchanged despite seismic shift in world. Yet even here, in sanctuary of home, things seem... off. Shadows pool in corners where they shouldn't, defying laws of light and space. Move through evening routine on autopilot – hang jacket, remove shoes, wash hands. Each action feels like performance, ritual enacted to maintain illusion of normalcy.

In bathroom, pause, staring at reflection. Skin seems paler than ever, almost translucent, black veins more pronounced. As I watch, swear I see them pulse, like living things beneath skin. For a moment, just a moment, think I see reflection smile – dark, hungry expression sending shiver of both fear and exhilaration down spine.

Shake head, make way to spare room. Soft glow of heat lamps welcomes me, along with quiet rustling of pets. Here, among scaled and chitinous friends, feel some tension leave body. Yet even this familiar space feels changed, charged with new energy can't quite name.

"Hey there," murmur, approaching ball python's enclosure. Snake raises head, tongue flicking out to taste air. Is it imagination, or do python's eyes seem to glow with inner light, reflecting newfound darkness within?

Open tank, gently lift python. Cool, smooth scales against skin comforting, grounding. Yet as hold snake, feel strange connection forming, as if darkness within reaching out, communing with creature's primordial nature.

As cradle python, mind wanders back to events of day. Strange call. Shadows seen, felt, manipulated. Should be terrifying, but instead, feel... curious. Excited, even. For first time in years, tomorrow holds promise of unknown, of power waiting to be explored and mastered.

After ensuring all pets fed and comfortable, each interaction charged with new, unspoken understanding, retreat to bedroom. Sit on edge of bed, staring at alarm clock. 9:45 PM. In normal routine, would be asleep in fifteen minutes, ready to wake at 5:30 AM and do it all over again.

But nothing feels normal anymore. Shadows in room seem to lean in, waiting, expectant.

Reach out, finger hovering over alarm switch. Events of day flash through mind – monotony of morning, strange incident with Mrs. Holloway, intense connection with frightened caller, collapse. Each memory tinged with darkness, with power that both thrills and terrifies.

Something has awakened inside. Something dark, ancient, utterly fascinating. World has changed, or perhaps I have changed, opening eyes to reality that has always existed just beyond veil of mundane.

Finger touches switch. Flicks it off.

For first time in three years, go to bed without setting alarm. Tomorrow will come with or without blaring 5:30 AM wake-up call. And for once, eager to see what it will bring, what new shadows will reveal themselves in light of day.

As drift off to sleep, swear I feel shadows in room reaching out, caressing like old friends welcoming home. In dreams, stand at edge of vast, dark ocean, waves of shadow lapping at feet. Darkness calls, promising power, knowledge, terrible, wonderful transformation.

And somewhere in distance, voice that is both mine and not mine calls my name, beckoning deeper into abyss.

Chirp. Another call. Swipe. Click. But this time, as consciousness fades, it's the shadows that answer, hungry for what tomorrow may bring.
